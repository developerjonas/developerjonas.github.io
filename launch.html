Pre-launch · now → July 3 Warm the audience 4 days
day 1 — today
List 30 targets manually
Find 30 founders actively raising on LinkedIn, X, or Reddit r/startups. Comment genuinely on their posts. No pitch yet.
day 2
Post the build story
Tweet/thread: "I built a DocSend alternative for $0 in 30 days. Launching July 4. Here's why and what it does differently." Tag @inksheetapp.
day 3
DM 10 warm leads
Send 10 personal DMs to founders you've already engaged with. Offer free early access. Ask: "Would this be useful for your raise?"
day 4 — July 3
Set up launch assets
Write Product Hunt listing draft. Prepare 3 screenshots. Write a 5-line cold email template. Get 5 friends to upvote on day one.
Launch · July 4–7 Go wide, go fast 72 hours
channels to hit (all free)
Product Hunt free
Launch at 12:01 AM PST. July 4 is historically lower competition — use it. Title: "Inksheet — Know the second an investor opens your deck"
Reddit free
r/startups, r/Entrepreneur, r/SaaS. Post as a founder sharing a tool you built — not an ad. "I built this because DocSend costs $65/seat"
X / Twitter free
Post launch thread. Tag founders who engage with pitch deck content. Use #buildinpublic and #IndieHacker to reach the right crowd.
LinkedIn free
Post a personal story: "I was tired of sending pitch decks into the void." Founders actively raising scroll LinkedIn daily.
HN / IndieHackers free
Post "Show HN: Inksheet – DocSend alternative with no per-seat pricing". IndieHackers milestone post for the #buildinpublic audience.
Founder Slack groups free
OnDeck, YC alumni Slack, Founders Cafe. Ask: "Would anyone try this free? I want 5 users to break it." People love being first.
Post-launch · July 8–31 Convert and learn 3 weeks
week 2
Do 10 customer calls
Every free user who shares a deck → DM them. Ask 3 questions: What were you doing before? What nearly stopped you from signing up? What's missing?
week 2–3
Convert free → paid
When a free user hits 3 active deals, send a personal email (not automated). Offer a 1-week trial of Founder. Follow up once.
week 3
Write the "what I learned" post
Publish a raw numbers post: signups, conversions, what broke. This gets 10x more reach than a product post and brings the next wave.
ongoing
Build in public weekly
Every Friday: 1 tweet with a real metric (users, decks shared, views tracked). Consistency beats virality for early-stage SaaS.
lean principles to live by
Traction before features. Every week ask: did we get a new paying user? If no, pause building and go talk to 5 people.
Manual before automated. Your first 10 deals should come from DMs, not funnels. Funnels come after you know what to say.
Kill features that nobody asks for twice. If 3 users don't mention the same thing, it's not a priority — even if it feels important.
Charge earlier than feels comfortable. Free users give feedback. Paying users give you signal about what actually matters.
